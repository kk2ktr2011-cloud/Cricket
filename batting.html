The Two Main Types

There are two main styles of batting in cricket. They are: Defensive Batting and Attacking Batting. Defensive batting focuses on protecting your wicket and staying at the crease. Attacking batting focuses on scoring runs quickly by playing aggressive shots.

How To Bat

  • Stand side-on to the bowler with your feet about shoulder-width apart.
  • Grip the bat comfortably with both hands, keeping your top hand firm and your bottom hand relaxed.
  • Keep your eyes fixed on the bowler throughout their run-up.
  • Hold the bat just behind your back foot, ready to move into position.
  • Stay balanced with your knees slightly bent.
  • Move your front foot towards the line of the ball as it approaches.
  • Keep your head over the ball to maintain balance.
  • Swing the bat in a straight line towards the ball.
  • Watch the ball onto the bat before looking where it travels.
  • Follow through naturally after making contact.
  • Call clearly with your partner before attempting a run.
  • Practice regularly to improve timing, technique, and confidence.

Front Foot Drive

  • Step forward towards the pitch of the ball.
  • Keep your head directly above your front knee.
  • Present the full face of the bat to the ball.
  • Keep your elbow high throughout the shot.
  • Strike the ball underneath your eyes for maximum control.
  • Follow through towards the direction you want the ball to travel.
  • Aim to keep the ball along the ground.
  • Focus on timing rather than power.
  • Maintain your balance throughout the shot.
  • Practice driving both straight and through the covers.

Pull Shot

  • Play this shot mainly against short-pitched deliveries.
  • Move onto your back foot quickly.
  • Keep your eyes level and watch the ball carefully.
  • Rotate your hips and shoulders as you swing.
  • Strike the ball around chest height.
  • Keep your wrists strong to control the shot.
  • Roll your wrists over the ball to keep it down when possible.
  • Finish with a controlled follow through.
  • Avoid attempting the shot if the ball is too high.
  • Practice against bowling machines or throwdowns before facing fast bowlers.

Cut Shot

  • Play this shot against short, wide deliveries outside off stump.
  • Move onto your back foot.
  • Create room by opening your shoulders slightly.
  • Keep your eyes fixed on the ball.
  • Swing the bat horizontally through the line of the ball.
  • Use soft hands to keep the shot under control.
  • Aim to place the ball behind square on the off side.
  • Keep your balance throughout the stroke.
  • Do not reach too far outside off stump.
  • Practice placement before trying to hit the ball hard.

Defensive Shot

  • Move your front foot towards the line of the ball.
  • Keep your head still and over the ball.
  • Present the full face of the bat.
  • Keep your hands soft to reduce the chance of edges carrying to fielders.
  • Play the ball as close to your body as possible.
  • Keep the bat vertical throughout the shot.
  • Drop the ball at your feet when possible.
  • Maintain your balance after contact.
  • Focus on protecting your wicket rather than scoring.
  • Develop patience and concentration during long innings.

Lofted Shot

  • Only attempt this shot when you are confident of the delivery.
  • Get to the pitch of the ball with your front foot.
  • Keep your head steady during the swing.
  • Swing through the line of the ball with a full bat face.
  • Generate power using your legs and body rotation.
  • Follow through high after contact.
  • Aim for gaps in the field rather than fielders.
  • Choose the right ball to attack.
  • Keep your balance to improve timing.
  • Practice controlled lofted shots before attempting them in matches.
